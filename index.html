Understanding

What Are STDs?

Sexually transmitted diseases (STDs), also called STIs, are infectious diseases mainly transmitted through sexual contact. They can be caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites.

🔬

How STDs Spread

  • Vaginal, anal, or oral sexual contact
  • Contact with infected blood
  • Sharing contaminated needles
  • Mother-to-child during pregnancy or birth
  • Breastfeeding (some infections)
⚠️

Why STDs Are Dangerous

Many STDs may not show symptoms in the early stages, which increases the risk of spreading infection unknowingly. STDs affect millions of people worldwide and remain a critical public health problem requiring education and awareness.

Common STDs

Major Sexually Transmitted Diseases

Understanding each disease is the first step to protection.

🎗️
HIV / AIDS
Human Immunodeficiency Virus

Symptoms

  • Fever & fatigue
  • Rapid weight loss
  • Frequent infections
  • Swollen lymph nodes

Diagnosis

  • ELISA test
  • Rapid HIV test
  • PCR test
💊 Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)
🦠
Syphilis
Treponema pallidum (Bacterial)

Symptoms

  • Painless sores (chancre)
  • Skin rash
  • Fever
  • Neurological complications

Diagnosis

  • VDRL test
  • RPR test
  • Dark-field microscopy
💊 Penicillin Antibiotics
🧫
Gonorrhea
Neisseria gonorrhoeae (Bacterial)

Symptoms

  • Pain during urination
  • Abnormal discharge
  • Pelvic pain
  • May be asymptomatic

Diagnosis

  • Urine test
  • Swab culture
  • PCR test
💊 Antibiotics
🔵
Chlamydia
Chlamydia trachomatis (Bacterial)

Symptoms

  • Burning during urination
  • Abnormal discharge
  • Often no symptoms
  • Pelvic inflammatory disease

Diagnosis

  • NAAT test
  • Urine test
  • Swab test
💊 Azithromycin / Doxycycline
🧬
HPV
Human Papillomavirus (Viral)

Risks

  • Genital warts
  • Cervical cancer
  • Throat cancer
  • Most common viral STD

Prevention

  • HPV vaccination
  • Regular Pap screening
  • Safe sexual practices
💉 HPV Vaccine Available
⚡
Genital Herpes
Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV-2)

Symptoms

  • Painful blisters / sores
  • Itching & tingling
  • Recurrent outbreaks
  • Flu-like symptoms initially

Diagnosis

  • PCR test
  • Blood antibody test
  • Viral culture
💊 Antiviral Therapy
Early Detection

Diagnosis of STDs

Early diagnosis is essential for successful treatment and preventing complications.

01

Blood Tests

Detect antibodies & antigens for HIV, Syphilis, Hepatitis

02

Urine Analysis

Screen for Chlamydia & Gonorrhea infections

03

Swab Tests

Collect samples from sores, discharge, or affected areas

04

PCR Testing

Polymerase Chain Reaction for precise pathogen detection

05

Rapid Diagnostic Tests

Quick results for HIV and Syphilis at point-of-care

06

Microscopy

Direct observation of pathogens under microscope

✨ Why Early Diagnosis Matters

✔ Prevents severe complications
✔ Reduces transmission to others
✔ Improves treatment success rates
✔ Protects sexual partners
✔ Lowers healthcare costs long-term
✔ Prevents mother-to-child transmission
Stay Protected

Prevention of STDs

Prevention is the most effective method to control sexually transmitted diseases.

🛡️

Use Condoms

Correct and consistent use of condoms significantly reduces STD transmission risk.

🏥

Regular Check-ups

Routine medical examinations help detect infections early, even without symptoms.

💉

Vaccination

Get vaccinated against HPV and Hepatitis B to prevent these viral infections.

🚫

Avoid Shared Needles

Never share needles or syringes to prevent blood-borne STD transmission.

📚

Education & Awareness

Knowledge about STDs empowers individuals to make safer health decisions.

💬

Open Communication

Discuss sexual health openly with partners and healthcare providers.

Medical Care

Treatment of STDs

Treatment depends on the type of infection — bacterial or viral.

Bacterial STDs

Treatable with Antibiotics

  • ✔ Syphilis — Penicillin
  • ✔ Gonorrhea — Ceftriaxone
  • ✔ Chlamydia — Azithromycin
  • ✔ Usually fully curable
  • ✔ Complete the full course
Viral STDs

Managed with Antivirals

  • ⚡ HIV — Antiretroviral Therapy
  • ⚡ Herpes — Acyclovir / Valacyclovir
  • ⚡ HPV — Monitored & managed
  • ⚡ Controlled but not fully cured
  • ⚡ Lifelong management may be needed

📋 Important Treatment Principles

  • ✔ Early treatment prevents complications
  • ✔ Follow the full treatment course prescribed
  • ✔ Sexual partners should also receive treatment
  • ✔ Avoid self-medication — consult a doctor
  • ✔ Regular follow-up testing after treatment
⚠️ Warning

Complications of Untreated STDs

Untreated sexually transmitted diseases can lead to severe health consequences.

🚫

Infertility

Permanent damage to reproductive organs

😣

Chronic Pain

Ongoing pelvic and abdominal pain

🎗️

Cancer Risk

HPV linked to cervical & throat cancers

🛡️

Immune Deficiency

HIV weakens the immune system severely

🤰

Pregnancy Issues

Complications for mother and child

🧠

Nerve Damage

Late-stage syphilis affects nervous system
